## What NOT to Do

1. **Do NOT create fake directory profiles** — Automatically generating profiles on 50+ directory sites is a black-hat SEO tactic that can result in penalties. It also clutters the web with inconsistent information about you.

2. **Do NOT fabricate social proof** — Fake reviews, fake testimonials, fake client names. Even one fabricated review, if discovered, destroys all credibility.

3. **Do NOT buy backlinks** — Paid links from "SEO agencies" that promise 100 backlinks for ₹5,000 are low-quality and can get your site penalized by Google.

4. **Do NOT create accounts you won't maintain** — An abandoned LinkedIn profile with outdated info is worse than no LinkedIn profile. An empty Twitter account with 3 tweets from 2024 signals inactivity.

5. **Do NOT use different identities** — Don't use "Aditya S." on one platform and "Aditya Singh" on another and "aditya_dev" on a third. Consistency helps search engines understand that all these profiles belong to the same person.

6. **Do NOT keyword-stuff your bio** — "Web Designer Delhi NCR India Freelance Developer Next.js React TypeScript Tailwind CSS GSAP Three.js Affordable Premium Best" is spammy, not professional. Use natural language.

7. **Do NOT claim a Google Business Profile if you don't have a physical location** — Google actively removes and penalizes fake business listings. Remote-only freelancers should rely on their website and organic search, not local business listings.
